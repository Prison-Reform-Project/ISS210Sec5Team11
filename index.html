About

We believe that prison reform should be pursued because it would lead to the overall betterment of our society, and we want to advocate for a positive change making things more fair for all. Some of us have family that have been directly affected by the horrors that is the prison/justice system in the United States and feel strongly about this issue. It also negatively effects minority groups and their treatment is disproportionate to that of their white counterparts in the justice system. For far too long has their experience been different and we believe that needs to change.

Families Against Mandatory Minimums logo

Families Against Mandatory Minimums is an organization dedicated to working towards a more equal justice system for individuals. Their mission statement is, "FAMM’s mission is to create a more fair and effective justice system that respects our American values of individual accountability and dignity while keeping our communities safe." The organization emphasizes fair treatment without regarding race, humane care in prison, and providing rehabilitation programs. FAMM has a contact system that allows you to to ask questions on their website while leaving your information for a later response. Additionlly, they can be called at (202) 822-6700 and are located at 1100 H Street NW, Suite 1000, Washington D.C, 20005.

Angela J. Davis on racial disparities in the criminal justice system.

The video we chose perfectly goes along with our intersectional issue because it addresses the many concerns of the justice system and more specifically minorities in the justice and prison system. The speaker is Angela J. Davis, and a lot of what she talks about pertains to men of color. There are a disproportionate amount of them being convicted and sentenced than their white counterparts. She says, "One in three african american men can expect to go to jail at some point in their lifetime." (Davis) She goes on to say that there is discretionary decision making going on in the justice system by judges, the police and prosecutors that produce racially motivated results. The video perfectly encapulsates how we feel about the issue and how we think things need to improve going forward.

Ableism in Prison Reform

This article talks about the difficulties of being someone who has a disability and being imprisoned. Also how people who have developed disabilities during their time in prison, don’t have the same legal representation and protection in the justice system as those who were disabled before they got there. This article also talks about the emotional, mental and physical damages solitary confinement has on people with disabilities and the consequences they have. The article also briefly mentions those who don't have proper protection in prison such as transgender people and women who have suffered from abuse.

Racial Disparities in Incarceration Rates Graph

This image shows a bar graph comparing the disparities in incarceration between different racial groups and separates these statistics based on gender. By looking at the image, you can clearly see that the rate of incarcerations is very imbalanced especially when realizing that the population spread between these groups is not proportionate to the rates of incarceration. These inconsistencies are caused by a variety of factors such as over-policing in low-income neighborhoods and policies which target these different minority groups specifically. For more information and ways to help fix these issues visit https://www.prisonpolicy.org/ .

Racial Makeup of Prisons Map

This map shows the difference, by state, of white to black prisoners. The map shows the disparity in the forms of ratios. The states with the highest disparity are New Jersey, Minnesota, and Wisconsin. The states with the lowest disparity are Alabama, Georgia, and Mississippi. More Data on racial disparity in prisons is available here https://www.usnews.com/news/best-states/articles/2021-10-13/report-highlights-staggering-racial-disparities-in-us-incarceration-rates
